"The Emperor and the Kite"

"El Emperador y la Cometa"




51. She attached the rope to the string of the stick-and- paper kite, and made her way across the treeless plain.



52. When she reached the tower, she called to her father.



53. But her voice was as tiny as she, and her words were lost in the wind.



54. At last, though, the emperor looked out and saw his daughter flying her kite.



55. He expected the tiny basket of food to sail up to his window as it had done each day.



56. But what should he see but the strand of vines and grass and long black hair.



57. The wind was raging above, holding the kite in its steely grip.



58. And the princess was below, holding tight to the end of the rope.



59.Although the emperor had never really understood the worth of his tiniest daughter before, he did now.



60. And he promised himself that if her plan worked she would never again want for anything, though all she had ever wanted was love.
